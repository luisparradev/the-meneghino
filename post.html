Shades of Orange; Fiesta, Turmeric, and Coral

 

Orange you glad we said orange!  This attention-grabbing color once read as loud or tacky is now praised for its versatility.  It transcends seasons - from a vibrant color to a mute terracotta for autumn style.  Orange has a long history and has different meanings in different cultures. For a long time, this color did not have a name and was simply called yellow red.  In Asian cultures it symbolizes transformation and is connected to the highest state of illumination.      

Orange provokes immediate reaction and is used to signify not only danger but create a sense of excitement.  Wear orange when you want to stand out but be approachable.

Shades of Red; Pink Peacock, Pressed Rose and Jester Red

 

When in doubt, wear red.  Red was the first color humans perceived after white and black.  It is very powerful and intense.  For millennia, wearing red clothes expressed legitimization of social status, political authority, religious rank, ancestry, and cultural identity.  Red dyestuff was particularly expensive because of its limited availability making red cloth more desirable.

In ancient Egypt red was associated with life, health and victory   Pink is a mixing of white and red and has both of its parent colors characteristics.  Pink is associated with love and understanding. Both pink and red are great colors for date night.
